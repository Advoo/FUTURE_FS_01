About Me

The story so far — still unfolding.

Currently pursuing a degree in Computer Science at University of the Western Cape (UWC). I am focused on building structured, scalable systems that translate ideas into practical, usable products. My approach is grounded in system thinking—understanding how components interact, optimizing for efficiency, and designing with long-term evolution in mind. Beyond technical implementation, I have a strong inclination toward design. I am interested in creating solutions that are not only functional but also refined, intuitive, and personally meaningful. This perspective led to the development of Novar, a concept shaped around delivering systems that feel deliberate, cohesive, and purpose-driven rather than merely operational. I work across the full lifecycle of software development—from conceptual modeling and architecture to implementation—while maintaining a clear emphasis on clarity, performance, and user experience. My goal is to contribute to environments where innovation is expected, systems are thoughtfully engineered, and products are built to endure. I am particularly motivated by opportunities that combine technical depth with creative direction, where engineering decisions directly influence how people interact with and experience technology.

My technical roots run deep in Artificial Intelligencen , but my interests are broad. I am fascinated by how AI can be integrated into products to enhance human capabilities and solve real-personal problems . I am actively exploring machine learning frameworks and techniques, with a focus on how they can be applied in practical, user-centric ways.

Beyond the terminal, I carry a camera. Through Stomy Photography, I've walked into rooms with entrepreneurs, founders, and creatives — documenting their workshops and events. Every lens shift taught me something a textbook couldn't.

I believe technology and community are not separate. That's why I actively contribute to GKSS at UWC and serve as a Secretary, helping to organise events and initiatives that connect students with opportunities. I'm also a proud recipient of the Moshal Scholarship, which has been instrumental in my academic journey. As part of that, I took on the role of Moshal Outreach Champion — because the next developer who changes things might just need someone who showed up first.
